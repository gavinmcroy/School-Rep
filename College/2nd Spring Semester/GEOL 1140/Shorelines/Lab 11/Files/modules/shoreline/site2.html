Site Two

Two more properties for evaluation are on Clayton Point, where numerous people have built cottages for weekend and vacation use. This is an area of rapid shoreline erosion and property owners have petitioned local government for assistance. Local government has not responded to their requests. They believe that areas that are primarily used for part-time housing do not deserve large expenditures for erosion control.

You have two maps, one from 2003 and one from 1988. These maps show how the shoreline has changed and where cottages have been built. You can easily estimate the rate of change by comparing the two maps.

Your job is to determine if either the Carter or Froughton property is at risk from shoreline erosion.
