Lava Mountain has been dormant for centuries, but the very fact that it exists is a testament to its eruption. It is a composite cone built by alternating eruptions of tephra and lava. The summit stands several thousand feet above the surrounding land, which is already high in elevation. With a thick snowcap through most of the year, the mountain is a source of meltwater that causes spring flooding, and has the potential of solving summer water shortages. Recent earthquake activity has prompted the surrounding residents to be concerned about their safety.
