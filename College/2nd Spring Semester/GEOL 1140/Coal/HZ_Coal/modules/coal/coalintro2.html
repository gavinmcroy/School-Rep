Norma Doyle has inherited a 200-acre farm from her uncle. She has hired you as a geological consultant because a coal company has contacted her about producing the coal beneath the farm. The company has long-term contracts to supply coal to the Amos Power Plant, located about 10 miles driving distance from the farm. They have offered her $500,000 for the right to mine and sell the coal beneath the property. She has come to you to ask if this is a fair price.

Ms. Doyle knows that her property is valuable. When the Amos Power Plant was built about 15 years ago, all of her uncle’s neighbors sold their coal to mining companies. Her uncle refused to sell. The coal beneath all of these neighboring farms has been mined out. Today, the plant is buying all of the coal it burns from mines in a coal field about 12 miles northwest of Crossroads.
