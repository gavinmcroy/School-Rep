Johnson Reservoir is a multipurpose facility with a primary mission of flood control. Built just five years ago, boating, camping and swimming are already popular. Fishing is starting to attract many people because the thousands of fingerling fish that were stocked during the first two years are now starting to reach legal size. Managing the reservoir has been a challenge because most of the runoff is tied to the spring snowmelt on Lava Mountain. It is hoped that a new snowpack monitoring system will lead to effective flood control and water-supply management practices.
