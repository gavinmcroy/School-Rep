Amos Power Station is a coal-fired facility that generates power for Hazard City and urban areas in the east of the state. One of the first multi-unit power stations in this region, it has been in continuous operation since the 1950's. This location was chosen because coals of the Fruitwood Formation were abundant nearby. The last of those coals are now being mined and coals produced at distant locations are now being delivered by rail and truck. A study is underway to determine if dredging can make the Clearwater River a navigable waterway.
